Read the comments before you clone.

> - **[Running Gemma 4 26B at 5 tokens/sec on a 13-year-old Xeon with no GPU](https://www.neomindlabs.com/2026/06/08/running-gemma-4-26b-at-5-tokens-sec-on-a-13-year-old-xeon-with-no-gpu/)** (251pts HN) — a tour de force of inference optimization · ~60GB of DDR3 RAM, quantized, CPU-only. > - **[SQLite should have Rust-style editions](https://mort.coffee/home/sqlite-editions/)** (150pts HN, Lobsters) — provocative proposal: what if SQLite adopted Rust's edition model for backwards-compatible language evolution? · Thoughtful exploration of database design philosophy. A sober reflection on what we keep piling on top of what.
>
> - **[Designing APIs for Agents](https://www.freestyle.sh/blog/opinion/designing-apis-for-agents)** (60pts HN) — Freestyle.sh on the real design patterns for agent-facing APIs · Not vague handwaving about "agents need different APIs." Specific, practical guidance: idempotency, pagination that agents can traverse, structured error responses, and why your REST API is probably hostile to non-human consumers.
>
> - **[AI Customer Support at Scale: The Travel Industry's Billion Bet](https://blog.bytebytego.com/p/ai-customer-support-at-scale-the)** (ByteByteGo) — grounded, practical engineering analysis of where AI actually works in production support pipelines · Follows the support pipeline from first principles, shows why a tail of cases resists automation regardless of model quality, and maps three architectural approaches. No hype — just what works and what doesn't.
