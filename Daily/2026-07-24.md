**Lesson — The Attention Mechanism: Your Agent's Eyes**

Before an agent can plan, use tools, or debug itself, one thing has to work: it must *see* the right things in its context. That mechanism is **attention** — and it's the single most important architectural idea in modern AI.

Here's the mental model in three layers:

**Layer 1: What attention does.** The model reads a sequence of tokens. For each token, attention answers: *"which other tokens matter most right now?"* It's not scanning linearly — it's looking at everything at once, assigning a weight to every relationship. The word "it" in a sentence pays more attention to "the cat" than to "the mat." That's attention.

**Layer 2: Q/K/V — the lookup.** Every token produces three vectors:
- **Query (Q):** "What am I looking for?" — the current token's question.
- **Key (K):** "What do I contain?" — every token's label for matching.
- **Value (V):** "What do I contribute?" — the actual information to pass forward if matched.

The operation: Q × Kᵀ (dot product) → softmax → weights × V. That's it. The softmax turns raw similarity scores into a probability distribution — every token contributes, but only the relevant ones contribute meaningfully.

**Layer 3: Multi-head.** One attention head might learn "subject-verb agreement." Another learns "pronoun resolution." Another learns positional patterns. Multi-head means running this in parallel with different learned projections — the model attends to different relationship types simultaneously. Concatenate, project, done.

**Why this matters for agents.** Your agent's context window is finite. Every token in the prompt costs compute. Understanding attention tells you: (a) the model attends to ALL tokens, but (b) it weights them — so where you place instructions and how you structure context directly shapes what the agent *sees*. This is the foundation of context engineering (Unit 6). Start here.
