## Assigned

### AI Agent Engineering — U1: LLM Foundations, Week 1

**Task: Explain the Transformer** (easy · conceptual fluency)

Before you can design agents, you need to understand the substrate they run on. Today is about building the *mental model* — no code, no implementation, just deep conceptual understanding.

**The big idea.** A transformer takes a sequence of tokens, gives each one a "vote" on what every other token means (attention), and stacks many such voting rounds (layers) to produce a rich representation. At inference, this happens in a single forward pass — all tokens at once, masked so each can only see the past. The context window is the model's working memory; attention is how it decides what in that memory matters right now.

**What to learn (in this order):**
1. 🎥 Watch **3Blue1Brown: Transformers & Attention** (full series on YouTube) — this builds the visual intuition for Q/K/V.
2. 📖 Read **Yao's "The Second Half"** (linked in your Syllabus) — frames *why* this course is eval-forward.
3. 📖 Read **AI Engineering (Huyen), ch. 1–2** — the engineering lens: what matters for building on top of models.
